## Why use this tool?
In secondhand trading, the hard part is often not the price itself but **how you say it**.
A message that feels too blunt can make the other person defensive, while one that is too soft can make your actual condition unclear.

This tool helps you quickly build ready-to-send messages for:
- price offers
- accepting a negotiation
- declining a negotiation
- hold / reservation requests
- bundle deal suggestions

## How it works
1. Choose your role: buyer or seller.
2. Pick the situation.
3. Set tone, target name, item name, and price condition.
4. Add item condition, meeting place, or extra notes if needed.
5. Copy one of the three generated versions.

## Good use cases
### 1) Sending a lower price offer
You can mention your offer price and trading availability without sounding too aggressive.

### 2) Declining a negotiation as a seller
You can keep your boundary clear while staying polite.

### 3) Asking for a short hold
You can combine hold requests with timing and deal conditions in one message.

## FAQ
### Does it tell me the best negotiation strategy?
No. It helps turn your chosen condition into a polite and clear message.

### Can I include both price and pickup details?
Yes. Add them in the condition, place, and extra note fields.

### What if the tone feels too stiff?
Switch tone or use a different output version, then adjust the wording slightly.
