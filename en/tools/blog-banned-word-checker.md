## When a banned word checker helps
Before publishing, scan wording that may look spammy: aggressive promotion, overpromising claims, contact solicitation, short URLs, too many hashtags, and repeated keywords. It works well as a draft review step for blogs, WordPress posts, store product descriptions, review articles, and other pages that rely on search visibility.

## Main risks it checks
### 1) Spam and promotional keywords
Detects obvious risk keywords and repeated promotional patterns, such as loan, gambling, adult content, free trial, guaranteed profit, or similar wording.

### 2) Medical, legal, and finance overclaims
Flags wording such as `100%`, `guaranteed`, `cure`, `always`, or `no risk` when it can sound too absolute for sensitive topics.

### 3) Abuse-like patterns
Finds mechanical repetition that often appears in low-quality promotional pages.

### 4) External links and contact prompts
Checks for phone numbers, messenger IDs, Telegram-style contact prompts, URLs, and short-link patterns.

## Why use it?
Search systems can treat strong low-quality or spam signals as a reason to reduce visibility.

- Search omission or lower exposure risk
- Ranking drop or delayed indexing
- Reduced trust in the blog or page

## How to use it safely
- Do not treat the scan as a search ranking guarantee. Review whether the whole paragraph reads naturally.
- The value and context of the article matter more than one isolated word.
- Replace flagged terms with clearer, more specific wording instead of simply deleting every detected word.
- For medical, legal, or finance topics, include conditions, evidence, and limits instead of absolute claims.

## FAQ
### Does finding a banned word always mean my post will be hidden from search?
No. A single word does not guarantee a search issue, but repeated or excessive spam signals can increase the risk of reduced visibility.

### Is a clean result enough before publishing?
No. The checker only catches common wording and pattern risks. You should still review context, usefulness, evidence, originality, and readability.

### Why are normal words sometimes detected?
Some ordinary words can become risk signals when they often appear in abusive or overly promotional documents, so they are flagged for review.

### Is my text stored on a server?
No. The scan runs inside your browser, and your draft is not sent to or stored on the server.

### What kind of writing should I check?
Use it before publishing blog reviews, product copy, health/legal/finance explanations, secondhand-sale posts, or any page where search visibility and trust matter.
