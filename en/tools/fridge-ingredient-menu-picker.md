## Why do you need a fridge ingredient menu picker?
A lot of people have ingredients at home but still cannot quickly decide what to make.
After work or right before grocery shopping, even searching through long recipes can feel tiring.

Common situations include:
- you only have everyday ingredients like eggs, onion, and tofu left
- you want to clear out the fridge but cannot think of a menu idea
- you need something fast and do not want a heavy cooking project
- the tools you can use right now are limited to a pan, pot, air fryer, or microwave
- you want to cook at home instead of ordering delivery, but the decision itself feels draining

This tool was made to reduce that friction.
If you choose **the ingredients you already have + meal context + cooking time + available cooking tools**, it organizes menu candidates you can realistically make now, along with missing optional ingredients.

## How does it work?
The input flow is simple.

1. Enter the ingredients you have at home with commas or line breaks.
2. Choose the meal context, such as breakfast, lunch, dinner, or late-night meal.
3. Select how much cooking time you have, such as 10 minutes, 20 minutes, or 30+ minutes.
4. Check which tools are available, such as a pan, pot, air fryer, or microwave.
5. Choose the direction you want, such as hearty, light, soupy, or spicy.

Then the tool scores menu ideas based on ingredient match and cooking constraints, and shows:
- **three menu ideas that are easy to make right now**
- **core ingredients you already have**
- **optional ingredients that would help**
- **why each menu fits your situation**

## Especially useful in these cases
### 1) When you want to clear out the fridge before shopping
If you want to use up leftover ingredients first, this helps you narrow down realistic menu ideas quickly.

### 2) When you want to solve one meal within 15 to 20 minutes after work
Because it reflects both cooking time and available tools, practical meal directions appear before long recipe hunting.

### 3) When you want to switch from delivery to home cooking but decide fast
Before looking up a full recipe, you can first choose a broad direction like “fried rice” or “soybean paste soup with tofu.”

## How to use it
1. Enter the ingredients you have at home using commas or line breaks.
2. Choose the meal context and cooking time you want.
3. Check the cooking tools you can use.
4. Choose the type of meal feeling you want.
5. Click `Get Menu Ideas`.
6. Copy the result and use it for notes or a grocery list.

## Example use cases
### Example 1) If you have eggs, onion, ham, and rice
- Meal context: dinner
- Cooking time: 10 to 20 minutes
- Cooking tool: pan
- Desired style: hearty meal

→ Ham vegetable fried rice, egg fried rice, or an omurice-style dish may rank high.

### Example 2) If you have tofu, green onion, soybean paste, and zucchini
- Meal context: dinner
- Cooking time: around 20 minutes
- Cooking tool: pot
- Desired style: warm / soupy

→ Soybean paste stew, tofu soybean paste soup, or stir-fried zucchini with soybean paste can fit well.

### Example 3) If you only have bread, cheese, and eggs
- Meal context: breakfast
- Cooking time: within 10 minutes
- Cooking tools: pan or microwave
- Desired style: light

→ French toast, cheese toast, or microwave egg bread can appear first.

## FAQ
### Is it more accurate if I enter more ingredients?
Usually yes. But you do not need to enter every seasoning or tiny detail. A few core ingredients are often enough to narrow the direction.

### Does it give full recipes?
The current version focuses more on **choosing menu candidates and organizing ingredient-use direction** than on detailed recipes. Once you decide the dish, you can continue with search or your own recipe.

### Can I use it for vegetarian or diet-friendly ideas?
Yes. Because candidates are filtered by your ingredient input and the meal direction you want, the results work better when you leave out meat ingredients or choose a lighter style.

The Fridge Ingredient Menu Picker is a **picker-style tool that quickly narrows down easy meal candidates based on ingredients you already have at home**.
