## Why a schedule coordination message generator helps
Scheduling sounds simple until you actually have to send the message.
If it is too short, it can feel blunt. If it is too long, the important time options get buried.
That becomes even more noticeable when you are messaging someone for the first time, writing in a team channel, or arranging an interview.

This tool is built to reduce that friction.
Choose the situation, tone, recipient label, and time options, and it generates **copy-ready scheduling messages** you can send right away.

## Especially useful in these situations
### 1) Proposing meeting times
It helps when you need to coordinate a kickoff, 1:1, review, or external meeting and want a message that is clear without sounding stiff.

### 2) Coordinating appointments or plans
For meals, consultations, hospital schedule changes, or casual plans, it helps you suggest times without overthinking the wording.

### 3) Interview or consultation scheduling
When courtesy matters, a polished draft makes it easier to send a professional message quickly.

## How it works
1. Choose the situation, such as a meeting, appointment, interview, consultation, or reschedule.
2. Select the channel and tone.
3. Enter the recipient name or label, the purpose, and your time options.
4. Add a reply deadline or extra note if needed.
5. Click generate to get short, standard, and more polite versions.

## How to use it
1. Enter the recipient label and scheduling purpose.
2. Add one or more time options, one per line.
3. Choose the situation, channel, tone, and whether to ask for alternate times.
4. Click `Generate Message`.
5. Copy the version that fits best and send it.

## FAQ
### Can I include several candidate times at once?
Yes. Put each option on its own line and the message will present them in a cleaner format.

### Can I use it without a recipient name?
Yes. If you leave the recipient blank, it will generate a more general opening.

### Should I send the result exactly as it is?
Usually yes, but for important external communication you should still confirm details like meeting links, location, and title one more time.
