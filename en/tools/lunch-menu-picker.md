## Why this lunch menu picker is useful
Choosing what to eat sounds simple, but it gets strangely harder when you are already hungry.
It is especially easy to waste time in situations like these:

- when you need to eat alone and get back to work quickly
- when it is a team lunch and everyone says “anything is fine”
- when you have a budget but no menu comes to mind
- when you already know the kind of food you want, like soup or a rice dish

This tool is built to cut down that hesitation.
Choose the meal situation, who you are eating with, your budget, and the kind of food you feel like, and it immediately shows three easy-to-share candidates.

## How it recommends menus
The logic is not a huge AI model. It is a lightweight scoring approach based on the factors that matter in real meal decisions.

1. **Meal time**: lunch / dinner / late-night
2. **Group type**: solo / 2-3 people / team lunch
3. **Budget range**: light / standard / flexible
4. **Mood**: hearty meal / light and clean / spicy and stimulating / quick bite / good for conversation
5. **Preference options**: include hot dishes, include rice-based dishes

It scores menu types against those conditions and shows the top candidates.
So rather than giving you a totally random pick, it works more like a **decision helper that makes the current situation easier to choose for**.

## Especially useful in these situations
### 1) Picking a company lunch menu
With team lunches, the real problem is often not the food itself but the **decision fatigue**.
Copy the result into a group chat and you can shorten the back-and-forth.

### 2) When you are eating alone and do not want to think too long
Once you set your budget and mood, menus that are easy to eat solo move higher.
It is especially handy during short lunch breaks.

### 3) When dinner feels undecided
It supports dinner and late-night use too, so it also works when you get home hungry and still do not know what to eat.

## How to use it
1. Choose the meal situation.
2. Choose who you are eating with.
3. Select your budget.
4. Pick the mood you want.
5. Choose whether to include hot dishes or rice-based dishes.
6. Click `Get Menu Suggestions` to generate the top three candidates.
7. Copy the result into notes or a messenger chat if needed.

## FAQ
### Can the tool ever show only a small set of options?
If the filters are extremely narrow, the candidate pool can shrink. In that case, the tool relaxes some conditions and recommends again.

### Does it support detailed filters like dieting or vegetarian choices?
The current version focuses on fast everyday decisions. More detailed dietary filters could be added later.

### Can we use it like a quick team vote?
Yes. You can copy the three results straight into chat and pick from them.

The Lunch Menu Picker is a **picker-type tool that quickly narrows today’s meal decision down to realistic options that fit your situation**.
