## Why use the Customer Support Message Generator?
When you need a refund, exchange, delivery check, billing correction, or bug report, it can be hard to decide **what details to include and in what order**. Too much emotion can hide the actual request, while a message that is too short often leads to follow-up questions and slower handling.

This tool turns your product or service name, issue, desired resolution, order details, and preferred tone into a **clear, polite support message that does not miss the essentials**.

## How to use it
1. Enter the product or service name you are contacting support about.
2. Choose the inquiry type, such as refund, exchange, delivery, payment, or error report.
3. Briefly describe what happened and what resolution you want.
4. Add optional details such as order number, purchase date, and available attachments.
5. Copy the generated text into a support chat, email, or inquiry form.

## What the message tries to include
- The product, service, order, or account context the support team needs first.
- A short factual description of the issue without unnecessary blame.
- The action you want, such as refund, exchange, redelivery, correction, or investigation.
- Supporting details like dates, screenshots, receipts, or error messages when available.
- A polite closing that asks for the next step or a response within a reasonable time.

## Especially useful when
### 1) Requesting a refund or exchange calmly
It keeps the message focused on purchase details, facts, and the requested action, so the support agent can check the case faster.

### 2) Reporting an app or website error
It prompts you to include where the error happened, when it happened, whether it can be reproduced, and whether screenshots are available.

### 3) Asking about delayed or missing delivery
It makes the next step—reshipment, refund, tracking check, or schedule confirmation—clear from the first message.

## FAQ
### Can I send the generated text as-is?
Usually yes, but confirm order numbers, dates, attachments, requested deadlines, and personal details before sending.

### Is this for legal dispute letters?
No. This is for ordinary customer support inquiries. For serious disputes, high-value losses, cancellation conflicts, or contract issues, seek appropriate professional or official help.

### Is being aggressive more effective?
Not usually. Clear facts, a concrete request, and a reasonable response deadline are often more useful than anger.
